Passion For Tech

I am definitely a computer nerd. I had an old desktop machine when I was very young, I saved up to buy my first laptop with birthday & Christmas money in 3rd grade, and my first powerful gaming laptop in 9th grade. I love all things information technology (IT). In High School, I had the opportunity to join the Information Technology & Software Engineering block program: a 2-hour a day class that dabbled in pretty much every area of IT. Topics such as programming, security, networking, software, hardware, operatting systems, etc.

My love for computers continued throughout my college career, in which I was able to complete dozens of projects spanning across the broad field of Computer Science. A couple of the later projects can be seen on my GitHub, although I am far more proud of the work I've done professionally than the simple projects completed for school.

Professional Experience Pre-Graduation

At the University of Cincinnati, the Computer Science degree follows the 5-year CEAS program's structure, which required us to take five professional Co-Op semesters. This gave me the opportunity to obtain five entire semesters of real-world, professional work experience before I graduate. Consequently, this is how I kickstarted my professional career. I've outlined my professional experience in more detail on the corresponding page of this portfolio.

Faith
